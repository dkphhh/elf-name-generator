### Drow Society and Naming

In Dungeons & Dragons, Drow society is matriarchal, ruled by priestesses of the Spider Queen Lolth. Names reflect this dark, spider-themed culture.

**Key Characteristics:**

- Harsh, exotic consonant combinations
- Apostrophes common (especially in house names)
- Gender-specific patterns
- House names indicate social status

### Male Drow Names

**Common Patterns:**

- Often include: z, x, k, v, th sounds
- End in: -en, -in, -on, -ar, -ath
- Typically 2-3 syllables

**Famous Examples:**

- **Drizzt Do'Urden** - The legendary ranger, rebel against drow society
- **Zaknafein** - Drizzt's father, weapon master
- **Jarlaxle** - Mercenary leader, rogue extraordinaire
- **Gromph** - Archmage of Menzoberranzan

**Generated Name Examples:**

- Zyn'rae Darkweaver

- Xal'atath Nightwhisper
- Rizzen Webwalker

### Female Drow Names

**Common Patterns:**

- Often softer than male names but still exotic
- End in: -ara, -yne, -ice, -viir, -afae
- Usually 2-4 syllables
- May include title prefixes (Matron, Priestess)

**Famous Examples:**

- **Malice Do'Urden** - Matron Mother of House Do'Urden

### Drow House Names

**The Format:** [First Name] [House Name with Apostrophe]

**Noble Houses:**

- Do'Urden
- Baenre
- Oblodra
- DeVir
- Hun'ett

**House Name Patterns:**

- Capital letter + apostrophe + lowercase
- Often 2-3 syllables after apostrophe
- Sound intimidating or mystical

**Creating House Names:**

- Use harsh consonants: D', Z', X', K', V'
- Add flowing second part: -urden, -aerne, -lodra
- Test: "Matron Mother of House \_\_\_"

## üåã Elder Scrolls Dunmer Names

### Dunmer Culture and Naming

Dark Elves in the Elder Scrolls (Dunmer) have a very different culture than D&D Drow. They're honorable, tradition-bound, and organized into Great Houses.

**Key Characteristics:**

- Vaguely Sumerian/Middle Eastern influences
- House names are crucial to identity
- More "realistic" sounding than D&D Drow
- Apostrophes rare or absent

### Male Dunmer Names

**Common Patterns:**

- End in: -th, -on, -ar, -en, -yn
- Often include: l, v, r, n sounds
- 2-3 syllables typical

**Famous Examples:**

- **Vivec** - Warrior-Poet god of Morrowind
- **Divayth Fyr** - Ancient wizard, over 4000 years old
- **Neloth** - Telvanni wizard in Skyrim
- **Jiub** - Saint who drove out the cliff racers

**Generated Name Examples:**

- Llethron Hlaalu
- Varyon Redoran
- Athyn Telvanni
- Severin Telendas

### Female Dunmer Names

**Common Patterns:**

- End in: -a, -is, -ra, -yn, -wen
- Softer sounds than male names
- Often melodious despite dark elf heritage

**Famous Examples:**

- **Almalexia** - Goddess and Tribunal member
- **Seryn** - Wise woman of the Ashlanders
- **Brara Morvayn** - Councilor of Raven Rock
- **Cindiri Arano** - Temple priestess

**Generated Name Examples:**

- Llirala Indoril
- Senyna Dres
- Brevasa Sadri
- Varona Telvanni

### Dunmer Great Houses

**The Five Great Houses:**

1. **House Hlaalu** - Merchants and diplomats
2. **House Redoran** - Warriors and honor-bound
3. **House Telvanni** - Wizards and isolationists
4. **House Indoril** - Temple and tradition
5. **House Dres** - Slavers and farmers (controversial)

**Name Format:** [First Name] [House Name]

**Examples:**

- Neloth Telvanni
- Bolvyn Venim (Redoran)
- Orvas Dren (Hlaalu)
